📱 Method 1: iPhone Camera Stream

Easiest — No Computer Needed

What You Need

  • iPhone with iOS 15+
  • YouTube app or Streamlabs app (free)
  • Your Sports Hub URL

Step-by-Step

  1. Open your Sports Hub and create a match at /desk
  2. On your iPhone, open the overlay page:
https://your-app.onrender.com/overlay?id=1
  1. Tap "🎥 Start Camera" — your rear camera activates
  2. The score bug appears on top of the camera feed
  3. Now use iPhone Screen Recording to capture everything:
    • Open Control Center (swipe down from top-right)
    • Long-press the Screen Record button
    • Select YouTube or Streamlabs as the broadcast destination
    • Tap "Start Broadcast"
  4. Your iPhone is now streaming the camera + live score to YouTube! 🎉

💡 Pro tip: Have a second person manage the score desk on another phone/tablet while you hold the camera steady.

💻 Method 2: OBS Studio (Computer)

Professional Quality

What You Need

  • Computer with OBS Studio (free — obsproject.com)
  • Camera (webcam, capture card, or phone as webcam)
  • YouTube stream key

Step-by-Step

  1. Open OBS Studio on your computer
  2. Add your camera source (webcam or capture card)
  3. Add a Browser Source with this URL:
https://your-app.onrender.com/overlay?id=1&nobg=1&minimal=1
  1. Set Browser Source size to 1920×1080
  2. The score bug appears with a transparent background — it overlays perfectly on your camera feed
  3. In OBS, go to Settings → Stream, select YouTube, paste your stream key
  4. Click "Start Streaming" — you're live! 🎉

💡 The ?nobg=1&minimal=1 parameters remove the background and extra controls, giving you a clean transparent overlay perfect for OBS.

Using iPhone as Camera for OBS

You can use your iPhone as a wireless camera for OBS:

  • macOS: Use "Continuity Camera" — just point your iPhone at the game
  • Windows: Use DroidCam or Camo app to connect your iPhone as a webcam
  • Capture Card: Connect a camera via HDMI capture card for best quality
